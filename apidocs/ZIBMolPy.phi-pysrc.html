  3  r""" 
  4  Everything related to the nodes' phi-function $\phi_i(\vec x)$. 
  5   
  6  Each node $i$ has a phi-function $\phi_i(\vec x)$, which determines for each 
  7  position $\vec x$ the membership to that node. 
  8   
  9  The phi-functions partition the internal coordinate space. For all $\vec x$ 
 10  holds the following condition: 
 11  \[  \sum_i \phi_i(\vec x) = 1 \] 
 12   
 13  The phi-function can be written as a node-dependend numerator $\chi_i(\vec x)$ divided by 
 14  a normalisation-factor: 
 15  \[ \phi_i(\vec x) = \frac{\chi_i(\vec x)}{\sum_j \chi_j(\vec x)} \] 
 16   
 17  This numerator is defined as: 
 18  \[ \chi_i(\vec x) = \exp(- \alpha \operatorname{dist}(\vec x, \vec q_i)) \] 
 19   
 20  The node's position $\vec q_i$ is stored in the internal-attribute of the L{Node}-object. 
 21  The $\alpha$-value is stored in the L{Pool}. 
 22  The dist-function depends on the type of coordinates used.       
 23  For details look at L{InternalCoordinate.sub}. 
 24   
 25  Please note, that not all nodes contained in the pool participate in the partition 
 26  of the internal coordinate space. 
 27  If a node participates in the partition is determined by the property L{Node.isa_partition}.   
 28  """
