  3  r""" 
  4  Everything related to the nodes' phi-function $\phi_i(\vec x)$. 
  5   
  6  Each node $i$ has a phi-function $\phi_i(\vec x)$, which determines for each 
  7  position $\vec x$ the membership to that node. 
  8   
  9  The phi-functions partition the internal coordinate space. For all $\vec x$ 
 10  holds the following condition: 
 11  \[  \sum_i \phi_i(\vec x) = 1 \] 
 12   
 13  The phi-function can be written as a node-dependend numerator $\chi_i(\vec x)$ divided by 
 14  a normalisation-factor: 
 15  \[ \phi_i(\vec x) = \frac{\chi_i(\vec x)}{\sum_j \chi_j(\vec x)} \] 
 16   
 17  This numerator is defined as: 
 18  \[ \chi_i(\vec x) = \exp(- \alpha \operatorname{dist}^2(\vec x, \vec q_i)) \] 
 19   
 20  Calculation of $\phi_i(\vec x)$ might lead to division by zero for large $\alpha$. In order to calculate $\phi_i(\vec x)$ numerically stable, it is helpful to cancel fractions and obtain: 
 21  \[ \phi_i(\vec x) = \frac{1}{\sum_j \exp(- \alpha (\operatorname{dist}^2(\vec x, \vec q_j) - \operatorname{dist}^2(\vec x, \vec q_i)))}= \frac{1}{1 + \sum_{j\neq i} \exp(- \alpha (\operatorname{dist}^2(\vec x, \vec q_j) - \operatorname{dist}^2(\vec x, \vec q_i)))} \] 
 22   
 23  This is an improvement since the denominator is always larger than one.  
 24   
 25  The node's position $\vec q_i$ is stored in the internal-attribute of the L{Node}-object. 
 26  The $\alpha$-value is stored in the L{Pool}. 
 27  The dist-function depends on the type of coordinates used.       
 28  For details look at L{InternalCoordinate.sub<ZIBMolPy.internals.InternalCoordinate.sub>}. 
 29   
 30  Please note, that not all nodes contained in the pool participate in the partition 
 31  of the internal coordinate space. 
 32  If a node participates in the partition is determined by the property L{Node.isa_partition<ZIBMolPy.node.Node.isa_partition>}.   
 33  """
